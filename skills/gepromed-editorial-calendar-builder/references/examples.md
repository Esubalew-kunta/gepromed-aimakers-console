# GEPROMED editorial calendar — worked examples

Calendars at target quality, showing the **two-layer model**: a `Fixed` immediate
week (concrete, ready to brief) and `Adjustable` remaining weeks (themes that can
move). Study the *moves*: pillars rotated, no two same-pillar posts back-to-back,
audiences mixed, event arcs built, every event/date/figure either real or
`[bracketed]`, owner is a role. A human validates before scheduling.

---

## 1 — EN · one-month LinkedIn plan, 2 posts/week, Bootcamp in week 2

**Assumptions:** month starting Mon 2026-06-22 · 2 LinkedIn posts/week (Tue+Thu) ·
balanced pillars · Vascular Bootcamp [date to confirm] in week 2.

### ═══ FIXED — immediate week (Week of 2026-06-22) ═══
| Date | Day | Channel | Format | Pillar | Audience | Title/Angle | CTA | Owner | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 2026-06-23 | Tue | LinkedIn | text | Explant Analysis | manufacturers | "An explant is evidence, not waste" | none | Comms | Fixed |
| 2026-06-25 | Thu | LinkedIn | event | Education | participants | Vascular Bootcamp — announce ([date]) | register [link] | Comms | Fixed |

### ═══ ADJUSTABLE — rest of the month ═══
| Week | Theme | Candidate topics | Channel(s) | Pillar focus | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Week of 06-29 | Bootcamp week | build-up/behind-the-scenes; recap with [N] participants | LinkedIn | Education | Adjustable |
| Week of 07-06 | Clinical Research | [publication] plain-language takeaway; surveillance angle | LinkedIn + newsletter | Clinical Research | Adjustable |
| Week of 07-13 | Testing | how a device is bench-tested; a carousel on the implant cycle | LinkedIn | Testing | Adjustable |

**Moves:** week 1 locked and concrete; Bootcamp arc (announce → during → recap)
spans the adjustable weeks; pillars rotate (Explant → Education → Clinical → Testing);
audiences differ; the Bootcamp date and participant count are bracketed.

## Anti-pattern gallery (never produce these)
- Locking a whole month rigidly with no adjustable layer.
- Leaving the current week vague instead of concrete and ready to brief.
- Two same-pillar posts back-to-back, or every post aimed at surgeons.
- Inventing an event date or a participant count instead of `[bracketing]` it.
- Assigning an owner who is a named individual instead of a role.
- A 5/week cadence the team cannot sustain.
