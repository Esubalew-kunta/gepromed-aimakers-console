# GEPROMED Editorial Calendar Builder — intake questions (FULL tier)

Ask **one batched round of at most 5** numbered questions before planning. Each
carries a suggested **default** or 2–3 options. Always offer the escape hatch.
**Skip any question the brief or `MEMORY.md` already answers.** Cap at 2 rounds,
then proceed on stated assumptions.

---

## The batched set (ask these, ≤5)

> A few quick questions so the calendar fits your rhythm (reply `go` for defaults):
>
> 1. **Timeframe & start date?** this week · this month · a custom range. When does
>    it start? *(default: a full month starting next Monday)*
> 2. **Cadence (posts per week) & channels?** cadence = 1 / 2 / 3 per week;
>    channels = LinkedIn · website-blog · newsletter. *(default: 2 LinkedIn posts/week)*
> 3. **Upcoming events or fixed dates?** Vascular Bootcamp, a publication, a
>    deadline, an institutional date — with the date. *(default: none — I'll bracket
>    any event and invent no dates)*
> 4. **Theme priorities?** which of the four pillars to lead with — Testing /
>    Education / Clinical Research / Explant Analysis — or "balanced". *(default:
>    balanced rotation across all four)*
> 5. **Primary audiences & language?** audiences = surgeons / manufacturers /
>    researchers / institutions / participants / partners / mixed; language = FR / EN.
>    *(default: mixed audiences; mirror your message's language)*
>
> Reply `go` and I'll proceed with the defaults above.

---

## Notes for the model
- The **immediate week is the fixed layer**: it must be concrete enough to brief.
  The **rest of the month is the adjustable layer**: themes and placeholders.
- If an event is named (e.g. Vascular Bootcamp) but the date is missing, **bracket
  the date** and build the surrounding arc on placeholders — never guess a date.
- Cadence defaults exist so the skill never stalls. State each inferred choice in
  the `Assumptions:` line.
- Never re-ask anything already in the brief or `MEMORY.md` (e.g. a standing
  Tuesday/Thursday slot pattern).
- Keep the plan **executable**: do not propose a cadence the team cannot sustain.
