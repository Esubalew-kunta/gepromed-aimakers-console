# GEPROMED editorial calendar — QA rubric (score before returning)

Self-score every calendar against this 100-point rubric. **Do not return a plan
that scores below 95.** If below 95, fix the failing criteria and re-score. Keep
the final score in the `QA:` line of the output.

| # | Criterion | Pts | Fail = 0 unless… |
|---|---|---:|---|
| 1 | **Brief fidelity** — right timeframe, cadence, channels, audiences | 12 | Matches the intake/brief |
| 2 | **Zero invented facts** — no invented events, dates, figures, publications | 15 | All specifics real or `[bracketed]` (**gate**) |
| 3 | **Two-layer model** — Fixed immediate week vs. Adjustable rest, visibly separate | 15 | Both layers present and labelled (**gate**) |
| 4 | **Fixed week is concrete** — date, channel, format, pillar, audience, title, CTA, owner | 12 | Every fixed item is brief-ready |
| 5 | **Adjustable layer is flexible** — themes + candidate topics, not over-specified | 8 | Themed, movable, lighter detail |
| 6 | **Cadence respected** — slots match the agreed posts/week; sustainable | 8 | Count + pattern correct |
| 7 | **Pillar rotation** — all four touched over a month; no two same-pillar back-to-back | 8 | Balanced rotation |
| 8 | **Audience & format mix** — varied across the plan, one primary audience per slot | 6 | Not all aimed at one audience/format |
| 9 | **Event arcs** — events get announce→during→recap where they fit; tied to a pillar | 6 | Arc built when an event is in range |
| 10 | **Brand fidelity** — on-brand themes/voice; anchored to patient safety + a pillar | 5 | Per brand-guidelines |
| 11 | **Owner roles** — every item owned by a role, never a named individual | 3 | Roles only |
| 12 | **Memory applied + safety flag** — MEMORY honored; clinical/data items flagged for RQ | 2 | No stored rule violated; flagged when applicable |

**Scoring guidance**
- Treat criteria **2 and 3** as **gates**: inventing facts, or collapsing the
  two-layer model, makes the plan not shippable regardless of total. Fix and re-score.
- A beautiful but unsustainable calendar still fails criterion 6.
- Round honestly. The goal is an executable, on-brand plan, not a flattering number.
- When you revise after a low score, note internally what you fixed; expose only
  the final `QA:` line.
