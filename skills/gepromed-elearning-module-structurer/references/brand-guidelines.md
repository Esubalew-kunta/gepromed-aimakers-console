# GEPROMED brand guidelines (for e-learning structure)

Scoped brand reference bundled with the E-learning Module Structurer skill. Ground
every course architecture in this. Full master kit lives in the repo `/brand`, but
this file makes the skill self-sufficient on upload.

## Brand essence
GEPROMED is **the medical-device hub for patient safety** — a non-profit
scientific authority across the full implant cycle. In education it must feel like
a **trusted scientific safety authority** running rigorous, simulation-based
training: clinical, precise, calm, credible, non-commercial. Course structures
should be pedagogically sound and safety-framed, never gamified marketing.

- **One-line essence:** Engineering safer medical-device care across the implant lifecycle.
- **Archetype:** Guardian + Expert — protect patients through technical knowledge.
- **Heritage:** founded 1993 (formerly GEPROVAS); based in Strasbourg; **René Kieny Education Center**.

## The four pillars — anchor: Education
1. **Testing** — mechanical / biomaterial testing of cardiovascular devices.
2. **Education** — simulation-based surgical training (vascular, ophthalmology, expanding). ← this skill's anchor
3. **Clinical Research** — device surveillance and clinical studies.
4. **Explant Analysis** — collecting and analyzing explants to close the safety loop.

E-learning modules can connect to the other pillars (e.g. a vascular module
referencing explant findings), reinforcing the safety loop as a teaching device.

## Proof points (use only when true and relevant; never invent)
- +1150 healthcare professionals trained since 2018
- Qualiopi certified · ISO 9001 and ISO 13485 certified
- Simulation-based training at the René Kieny Education Center
- World's largest database of vascular explants (a teaching asset)

> Do not bake unverified figures (completion rates, satisfaction scores, hours)
> into a course structure. Bracket them for the Education team to confirm.

## Values
Independence · transparency · sharing · ethics · humanism · safe care · risk
prevention · scientific rigor · continuous improvement.

## Audiences (learners)
Residents · qualified surgeons / HCPs · researchers · mixed cohorts. Match
objective difficulty (Bloom level) to the learner level.

## Positioning guardrails
- **Do** design as: rigorous, safety-first, simulation-anchored, clinically credible training.
- **Do NOT** design as: a generic MOOC · gamified edutainment · marketing dressed
  as a course · clinical guidance issued by GEPROMED (content is SME-authored/validated).
