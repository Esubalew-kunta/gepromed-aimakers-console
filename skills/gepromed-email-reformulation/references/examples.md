# GEPROMED email reformulation — worked examples

Real before→after rewrites showing the target quality. These represent the
**GEPROMED house voice** (company-wide, not personal). Study the *moves*:
language mirrored, intent preserved, facts untouched, hype removed, one clear
ask, recipient-appropriate register, unknowns bracketed.

---

## 1 — FR · follow-up to a training participant (warm-professional)
**Before:**
> bonjour, suite a votre demande pour la formation bootcamp vasculaire je voulais
> savoir si vous etes toujours interessé?? on a encore de la place. dites moi
> vite svp sinon ça part. merci

**After:**
```
Subject: Votre place au Bootcamp Vasculaire — confirmation

Bonjour [Prénom],

Suite à votre demande d'inscription au Bootcamp Vasculaire, je reviens vers vous
pour savoir si vous souhaitez confirmer votre participation.

Des places sont encore disponibles pour la session du [date]. Je peux vous
réserver la vôtre — il me faudrait simplement votre confirmation avant le
[date limite].

Je reste à votre disposition pour toute question sur le programme ou la logistique.

Bien cordialement,
[Nom]
GEPROMED — René Kieny Education Center
```
**Moves:** removed pressure, kept real facts, deadline bracketed, concrete next step.

## 2 — EN · outreach to a medical-device manufacturer (independent, evidence-led)
**Before:**
> Hi, we are GEPROMED, a world-class leader in explant analysis 🚀 and we would
> love to partner with your amazing company to test your devices, we are the best
> in europe!! let me know asap

**After:**
```
Subject: Independent explant analysis & mechanical testing — GEPROMED

Dear [First name],

I am reaching out from GEPROMED, a non-profit medical-device hub focused on
patient safety. We run independent explant analysis and mechanical/biomaterial
testing on cardiovascular devices, under ISO 9001 and ISO 13485.

If useful, I would be glad to share how our testing and explant analysis could
support your device evaluation work, and to explore whether a collaboration makes
sense.

Would you be open to a short call in the coming weeks?

Best regards,
[Name]
GEPROMED — Test Platform
```
**Moves:** hype/emoji stripped; "best/world-class" replaced by verifiable facts; one ask.

## 4 — EN · institution / funder (formal, mission-forward)

> loads of people came, and we'd love more funding to do more of this great stuff.

**After:**
```
Subject: [Programme] outcomes and continued support

Dear Sir or Madam,

I am writing to share the outcomes of our recent [programme] and to discuss its
continuation.

The session trained [N] healthcare professionals in [specialty], contributing to
GEPROMED's mission of improving patient safety through simulation-based surgical
education. [Add one concrete outcome / satisfaction figure if available.]

Building on these results, we would welcome the opportunity to discuss continued
support to extend the programme to [scope]. I would be glad to provide a detailed
report and to meet at your convenience.

Yours faithfully,
[Name]
GEPROMED
```
**Moves:** formal register/closing; vague enthusiasm replaced by bracketed metrics.

## 9 — FR · demande d'information précise (easy to answer)

Dans le cadre de l'analyse en cours, pourriez-vous nous transmettre les
informations suivantes concernant les explants envoyés :
- [date d'explantation]
- [référence / type de dispositif]
- [contexte clinique, si disponible]

Cela nous permettra de finaliser l'analyse, idéalement d'ici le [date].

Bien cordialement,
[Nom]
GEPROMED — Analyse d'explants
```
**Moves:** vague "des infos" → an itemized, answerable list; reason + date.

The full dataset and methodology are available for your review. We would suggest

[Name]
GEPROMED — Test Platform

[Note for sender: technical/regulatory content — have RQ review before sending;
confirm all values.]
```
**Moves:** no euphemism, no blame; facts bracketed for confirmation; routed to RQ.

## Anti-pattern gallery (never produce these)
- Keeping "🚀", "world-class", "amazing", "best in Europe", "super well".
- Uniform sentence length that reads machine-generated.
- A polite rewrite with **no subject line** and **no explicit ask**.
- Inventing numbers ("we trained 2000 people") instead of bracketing `[N]`.
- English closings on a French email; institutional formality on an internal note.
- Mirroring the *sender's* casual quirks instead of the GEPROMED house voice.
- Sending sensitive/regulated content without a validation flag.
