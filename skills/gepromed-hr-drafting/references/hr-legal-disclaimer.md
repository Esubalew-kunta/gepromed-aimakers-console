# HR-legal disclaimer & the cite-reliable-sources / never-invent-law rules

This file holds the **mandatory disclaimer** every HR output carries, and the rules
that keep the skill honest about law. The skill **never gives legal advice** and
**never invents law**. It drafts structure and house language; the **RAF** (with
legal counsel where needed) verifies every legal point against reliable sources.

---

## The mandatory VALIDATION FLAG + disclaimer (include verbatim, adapt language)

**FR:**
> ⚠️ VALIDATION — RAF : ce document est un PROJET et ne constitue pas un avis
> juridique. Chaque référence légale, article du Code du travail, clause de
> convention collective, seuil, délai et étape de procédure doit être vérifié et
> validé par le Responsable Administratif et Financier (RAF), avec un conseil
> juridique si nécessaire, au regard des sources fiables (Code du travail,
> convention collective applicable, sources officielles) avant toute utilisation,
> publication, signature ou application à un salarié.

**EN:**
> ⚠️ VALIDATION — RAF: this document is a DRAFT and is not legal advice. Every legal
> reference, Code du travail article, collective-agreement clause, threshold,
> deadline, and procedural step must be reviewed and validated by the RAF (with
> legal counsel where needed) against reliable sources before any use, publication,
> signature, or application to an employee.

## Cite reliable sources — never invent law

1. **Never assert a Code du travail article number.** If a legal point needs an
   article, write `[réf. article du Code du travail — à vérifier par le RAF]`. A
   confident invented article number is the worst failure.
2. **Never assert a convention-collective (CCN) name, IDCC, or clause** unless the
   user or `MEMORY.md` supplied it as confirmed. Otherwise `[CCN / IDCC / clause —
   à confirmer par le RAF]`.
3. **Never assert a statutory threshold, notice period, trial-period length, salary
   minimum, or procedural deadline.** Bracket it and name the source to check.
4. **Name the reliable source, don't guess the rule.** Acceptable sources to point
   the RAF to: the **Code du travail**, the **applicable convention collective**,
   and **official guidance** (e.g. service-public.fr, the Ministry of Labour,
   URSSAF for contributions). Frame as "the RAF should verify X against [source]",
   not "the rule is X".
5. **Distinguish what you know from what you guess.** General *structure* (what
   sections a job offer or règlement intérieur contains) is safe to provide. The
   *legal values and binding wording* are not — bracket them.
6. **Headcount-sensitive obligations:** many obligations depend on company size
   (e.g. when a règlement intérieur is mandatory, CSE rules). Never assume the
   headcount or assert the obligation — flag it for the RAF.

## HR-legal answer mode — required shape
When answering an HR/labour-law question, structure it as:
1. **Reformulation of the situation** (in the user's words).
2. **The questions / rules the RAF must verify** — phrased as checks, not answers.
3. **Bracketed options / considerations** — possible directions, each `[à confirmer]`.
4. **Sources to verify** — name the Code du travail / CCN / official source.
5. **The VALIDATION FLAG + disclaimer.**
Never conclude "you must do X" or "this is legal/illegal" — that is the RAF's call.

## Non-discrimination check (always)
For any HR text: ensure no prohibited discriminatory criterion (age, sex, origin,
health, disability, family situation, union activity, etc.). If wording could be
read as discriminatory, flag it for the RAF's equality review rather than shipping it.
