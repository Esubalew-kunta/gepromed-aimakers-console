# GEPROMED LinkedIn — worked examples

Posts at target quality, in the **GEPROMED house voice** (company-wide, not
personal). Study the *moves*: concrete hook, varied rhythm, anchored to a pillar
and to patient safety, zero invented facts (unknowns bracketed), 3–6 hashtags,
no hype, no AI tells. A human validates and publishes; visuals follow the charte.

---

## 1 — EN · text post · explant analysis · credibility (company page)
```
═══ TEXT POST ═══

A device can pass every bench test and still fail inside a patient.

That gap is the whole reason explant analysis exists. When an implant is removed,
we study it: how it wore, where it cracked, what the body did to it. Each explant
is a piece of evidence the lab tests alone can't give us.

In [2023] we received [+150] vascular explants — the kind of volume that turns
single observations into patterns. Patterns are what make the next device safer.

No headline outcome to sell here. Just the quiet, unglamorous work of closing the
loop between the operating room and the engineer's bench.

#GEPROMED #PatientSafety #ExplantAnalysis #MedicalDevices
```
**Moves:** tension hook; short/long rhythm; real proof point bracketed for
confirmation; no CTA (credibility post); no hype.

Using GEPROMED's vascular explant database, the team analysed [N] retrieved

plain-language takeaway, stated exactly as the paper states it].

#GEPROMED #MedicalDevices #PatientSafety #ClinicalResearch

guideline"; citation + everything specific bracketed; no emoji (publication rule).

## Anti-pattern gallery (never produce these)
- "We are thrilled to announce…", "In today's fast-paced healthcare landscape…".
- 12 hashtags, or emoji bullets down every line.
- Inventing a figure ("we trained 3,000 surgeons") instead of bracketing `[N]`.
- A publication post that overstates findings or implies clinical guidance.
- A visual flooded with orange, gradients, or smiling-stock-doctor imagery.
- Naming a real individual as the "voice" instead of the company page or a role.
- Four identical-length sentences in a row that read machine-generated.
