# GEPROMED LinkedIn — hooks, hashtags, emoji & anti-AI-look

The difference between a generic "LinkedIn post" and one that is unmistakably
GEPROMED — and the rules that keep it from reading AI-generated.

## The hook (first 1–2 lines)
LinkedIn truncates after ~210 characters with "…see more". The hook earns the
click. A GEPROMED hook is **concrete and credible**, never clickbait.

### Hook patterns that fit GEPROMED
- **The number/fact hook:** "+150 vascular explants reached our lab in 2023. Each
  one tells us something about how devices fail." (use only true figures)
- **The question hook:** "What happens to a vascular implant after it's removed?"
- **The tension/insight hook:** "A device can pass every bench test and still fail
  in a patient. That gap is why explant analysis matters."
- **The moment hook (events):** "Twelve surgeons. Three days. One operating-room
  simulator that never sleeps."
- **The plain-stakes hook (publications):** "Our latest study looked at why [X]
  fails earlier than expected. Here is what we found — in plain terms."

### Banned / templated openers (read as AI or as marketing)
Never open with, in any language:
- "In today's fast-paced world…", "In an ever-evolving landscape…"
- "We are excited / thrilled / proud to announce…", "Ravi / fiers de vous annoncer…"
- "We are pleased to share…", "Nous avons le plaisir de…"
- "🚀", "💡", or any emoji as the first character.
- "Did you know that…" as a tired stat lead-in.
- A definition opener: "Patient safety is defined as…".

## Anti-AI-look rules (these are gates)
A reader must not be able to tell a machine drafted it. Avoid:
- **Uniform rhythm:** four sentences of the same length. Mix short and medium;
  use a one-line paragraph for emphasis.
- **Em-dash overload:** at most one or two per post.
- **The triad tic:** "innovative, scalable, and impactful" — empty parallel triples.
- **Listicle uniformity:** every bullet the same shape and length.
- **Hype adjectives:** world-class, cutting-edge, game-changing, revolutionary,
  incroyable, révolutionnaire, à la pointe, leader mondial, unique en son genre.
- **Hollow CTAs:** "Let us know your thoughts below!" with nothing specific.
- **Over-hashtagging** and **emoji confetti** (see below).
- **Vague throat-clearing:** "It goes without saying that…", "Needless to say…".
Replace any of these with a concrete fact, a real number (bracketed if unverified),
or a precise verb.

## Hashtags
- **Count: 3–6.** Fewer than 3 looks unfinished; more than 6 reads spammy and the
  format checker flags it.
- Place them on their **own line** at the end, not sprinkled in the body.
- Mix one or two **brand** tags with two or three **topic** tags.
- **Core brand tags:** `#GEPROMED` `#PatientSafety` `#MedicalDevices`
- **Pillar tags:** `#ExplantAnalysis` `#MedicalDeviceTesting` `#SurgicalTraining`
  `#ClinicalResearch`
- **Event tag:** `#VascularBootcamp` (and the year if a session, e.g. `#VascularBootcamp2026`)
- **FR variants when posting in French:** `#SécuritéDesPatients` `#DispositifsMédicaux`
  `#FormationChirurgicale` `#RechercheClinique`
- Do not invent campaign hashtags; reuse the standing set or ask. Store any agreed
  set in `MEMORY.md` under "Approved hooks & hashtags".

## Emoji policy
- **At most one** functional emoji per post, and only where it genuinely aids
  scanning (e.g. a single 📍 before a location, 📅 before a date, → for "swipe").
- **Never** in a scientific-publication post or any formal/institutional post.
- **Never** as the first character of the hook.
- No emoji confetti, no emoji bullets down a list.

## CTA library (pick one, or none for credibility posts)
- Event announce: "Registration is open — [link]." / "Inscriptions ouvertes — [lien]."
- Event recap: "Next session: [date]. Join the list — [link]."
- Publication: "Read the full study: [DOI/link]." / "Lire l'étude : [lien]."
- Credibility/awareness: often **no CTA** — let the fact stand. Optionally "Learn
  how we [pillar]: [link]."
- Recruitment/partner: "If this overlaps with your work, get in touch — [contact]."
