# GEPROMED outreach — worked examples

Real worked outreach drafts showing target quality and the **GEPROMED house voice**
(company-wide, not personal). Study the *moves*: one real hook, lead with the
target, one proof point, one low-friction ask, independent (not vendor) tone, human
rhythm, unknowns bracketed. FR + EN.

---

## 1 — EN · LinkedIn DM to a device-manufacturer R&D lead (test-platform / explant)
**Intake:** target = R&D lead at a peripheral-stent maker, recently presented on
nitinol fatigue; objective = test-platform/explant collaboration; angle = Testing +
Explant Analysis; channel = LinkedIn DM; tone = professional-independent.

**Draft:**
```
Channel: LinkedIn DM

Hi [First name],

Your talk on nitinol fatigue in peripheral stents caught my attention — it sits
close to what we see in explanted devices.

I work with GEPROMED, an independent non-profit medical-device hub. We run
mechanical and biomaterial testing under ISO 13485 and hold the largest database of
vascular explants, so we sometimes have real post-market signal that complements
bench fatigue data.

Would a short exchange on whether that's useful for your design or surveillance work
be of interest?

Notes: confirm the talk reference is accurate; bracket if unsure. [sender designation]
QA: 98/100
```
**Moves:** real hook first; one proof point (explant database); independent framing;
one soft ask; no calendar link.

## 2 — FR · note de connexion LinkedIn à un chirurgien vasculaire (formation)
**Intake :** cible = chirurgien vasculaire, intervenant à un congrès régional ;
objectif = formation/faculté ; angle = Education ; canal = note de connexion ;
ton = pair-à-pair.

**Draft :**
```
Channel: LinkedIn note (≤300 caractères)

Bonjour Docteur [Nom], votre intervention au [congrès] sur [sujet] rejoint nos
travaux de formation par simulation au René Kieny Education Center. Au plaisir
d'échanger sur d'éventuelles collaborations pédagogiques.

Notes : vérifier le nom du congrès et le sujet ; à compléter par l'expéditeur.
QA : 97/100
```
**Moves :** une accroche réelle, registre pair-à-pair, aucune tonalité commerciale,
sous 300 caractères, pas de demande lourde.

## 3 — EN · cold email to a researcher (collaboration)
**Intake:** target = PI in cardiovascular biomechanics, published on graft failure
modes; objective = research collaboration; angle = Clinical Research + Testing +
explant database; channel = email; tone = collegial-technical.

**Draft:**
```
Channel: Email
Subject: Explant data + testing for graft-failure research

Dear Prof. [Name],

Your recent work on [graft failure mode / paper] overlaps closely with data we hold
at GEPROMED, a non-profit medical-device hub for patient safety.

We maintain the largest database of vascular explants and run independent
mechanical/biomaterial testing — both of which could support a joint study on
[specific question]. We would be glad to explore whether a collaboration, or a
shared dataset, is useful for your group.

Would a brief exchange in the coming weeks be of interest?

With best regards,
[Name]
GEPROMED — Clinical Research

Notes: confirm the cited paper and the specific shared question before sending.
QA: 98/100
```
**Moves:** accurate reference to their work; collegial register; concrete shared
asset; one ask; bracketed specifics.

## Anti-pattern gallery (never produce these)
- "I hope this email finds you well / I came across your profile / I wanted to reach
  out" — automated tells.
- A fabricated hook ("I loved your recent post about X") when no such post was given.
- Generic flattery: "your impressive work", "your amazing company".
- A vendor pitch in sentence one; GEPROMED before the target's relevance.
- Three asks, or a calendar link in a first cold message.
- Listing every proof point — reads salesy; use one relevant point.
- A message that could be sent unchanged to 500 people (fails the blast test).
- Hype/superlatives ("world-class", "leader mondial") or emojis in a formal approach.
