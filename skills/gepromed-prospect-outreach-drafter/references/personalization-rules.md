# GEPROMED outreach — personalization rules (make it specific, evidence-led, human)

The difference between a message that gets a reply and one that gets deleted. These
rules make a GEPROMED outreach **specific, credible, and unmistakably written by a
person** — never an automated blast. **Personalization rests on real, supplied
evidence about the target. Never fabricate a hook.**

---

## 1. The one-hook rule
Every message needs **exactly one genuine, specific hook** about the target —
something true and supplied (their recent paper, a device line, a talk, a role, a
shared event, a publication). One real hook beats three vague compliments.
- Good: "Your group's work on [specific stent design / paper title] overlaps with…"
- Bad: "I came across your impressive profile and your great work in the field."
- **If no real hook is supplied, bracket it** — `[reference their recent work on X]`
  — and tell the sender to fill it. Do **not** invent "I saw your post about…".

## 2. Lead with them, not with us
Structure: **their relevance → why GEPROMED is useful to them → one small ask.**
GEPROMED's offer appears in sentence two or three, framed as useful *to the target*,
never as a pitch in sentence one. Open on the target; close on a low-friction ask.

## 3. Evidence over adjectives
Replace claims with verifiable facts (from `icp.md` proof points, only when true):
- Not "we are leaders in explant analysis" → "we maintain the largest database of
  vascular explants and run independent ISO 13485 testing."
- Use at most **one** proof point, the one relevant to this ICP. More reads salesy.

## 4. The anti-automation checklist (reads human, not merged)
Avoid every tell of an automated/templated message:
- No "I hope this email finds you well", "I came across your profile",
  "I wanted to reach out", "As you may know", "par la présente".
- No obvious merge-field shape ("Hi {FirstName}, I see you work at {Company}").
- Vary sentence length deliberately; one short sentence breaks the template feel.
- One idea per sentence; no stacked clauses that read generated.
- No generic flattery ("amazing work", "impressive company").
- A real person would not list three asks — keep **one**.

## 5. One low-friction ask
Make replying easy and cheap:
- "Would a short call in the coming weeks be of interest?"
- "Open to a quick exchange on whether this is relevant to your work?"
- Not "Let me know your thoughts" (vague) and not "Book 30 minutes here" (high
  friction, vendor-like). No calendar links in a first cold message unless asked.

## 6. Channel discipline
- **LinkedIn connection note:** ≤ ~300 characters. One hook + one soft reason to
  connect. No pitch, no link, no ask for a meeting yet.
- **LinkedIn DM:** 3–5 short sentences. Hook → relevance → one soft ask.
- **Email:** specific subject (no clickbait); 4–7 short lines; hook → relevance →
  one ask → signature. Slightly more context than a DM, still tight.

## 7. Tone by ICP (cross-reference `icp.md`)
- Manufacturer: professional, independent, evidence-led — *partner, not vendor*.
- Surgeon/HCP: peer-to-peer, concise, no marketing, respect their time.
- Researcher: collegial, technical, precise; reference their work accurately.
- Institution/funder: formal, mission-forward, accountability-led.

## 8. Independence & ethics
- GEPROMED is independent and non-profit; never imply endorsement of the target's
  product or a clinical outcome.
- Be transparent about who is writing and why. No dark-pattern urgency.
- No pricing or commitments in a first message; bracket if needed.

## 9. Honesty rules
- Never fabricate a shared history, a referral, a prior meeting, or the target's
  details. If unsure it is real, bracket it.
- Never invent GEPROMED facts, numbers, or certifications.
- Unknowns → `[bracketed placeholder]` for the sender to confirm.

## 10. The "could-this-be-a-blast?" test (final gate)
Before returning, ask: *could this exact message be sent unchanged to 500 people?*
If yes, it is not personalized — add the real, specific hook (or bracket it) and
cut the generic lines. A message that survives this test is shippable.
