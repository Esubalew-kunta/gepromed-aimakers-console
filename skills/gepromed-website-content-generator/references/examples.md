## 1 — EN · Authority page: Vascular explant analysis (pillar: Explant Analysis)

**Brief:** explant analysis page, audience manufacturers + clinicians, proof:
world's largest vascular-explant database, +150 explants in 2023, ISO 13485.
Keywords: primary "vascular explant analysis", secondary "device failure
analysis", "explant retrieval".

```
Assumptions: authority page · audience manufacturers + clinicians · EN · primary keyword "vascular explant analysis"

— PAGE: /en/explant-analysis —

Meta title:        Vascular Explant Analysis | GEPROMED
Meta description:   Independent vascular explant analysis from the world's largest explant database — helping manufacturers and clinicians improve device safety.

H1: Vascular explant analysis

[Intro]
Explant analysis is the examination of medical devices removed from the body to
understand how and why they fail. At GEPROMED, we perform it independently on
cardiovascular devices, turning each retrieved implant into evidence that makes
the next one safer.

H2: What we do
We receive, document, and analyse explanted vascular devices under ISO 13485,
combining mechanical examination with biomaterial assessment. Every explant is
catalogued in our database — the world's largest collection of vascular explants —
so findings can be compared across devices, indications, and time in the body.

H2: How explant analysis closes the safety loop
1. An explanted device is sent to GEPROMED with its clinical context.
2. We document, image, and analyse it against the failure question.
3. Findings feed clinical research and device surveillance.
4. Lessons return to manufacturers, clinicians, and training — so the next
   generation of devices and procedures is safer.

H2: Why it matters
A device failure seen only once is an incident. Seen across a large database, it
becomes a signal — one that can be acted on before it reaches more patients.
Independent explant analysis is how that signal is found.

H2: Who we work with
- Manufacturers — independent post-market evidence on real-world device behaviour.
- Clinicians — answers on a specific explant and the wider pattern behind it.
- Researchers — access to a structured explant database for studies.

[CTA]
Send an explant for analysis → [contact / submission page]
Questions about the process? Contact our explant analysis team.

Notes: Pillar = Explant Analysis. Confirm "+150 explants in 2023" before adding a figure to the page; ISO 13485 confirmed in brand kit. Internal links: homepage, testing page, clinical studies. Suggested alt text for hero: "GEPROMED engineer examining an explanted vascular device".
QA: 97/100
```

**Moves:** intro defines the term (GEO), proof replaces adjectives, the loop is a
numbered extractable passage, CTA is specific, unconfirmed figure bracketed.

## Anti-pattern gallery (never produce these)
- "GEPROMED is a world-class leader in innovative medical solutions" — hype + no proof.
- Two H1s, or H2 → H4 skips, or empty clever headings.
- A meta title of 90 characters, or a description that is a keyword list.
- Inventing "98% satisfaction" or a partner name to fill a Qualiopi/proof field.
- A generic "Learn more" CTA with no destination.
- A translated-sounding French page that mirrors English syntax word for word.
- A training page missing accessibility, pricing, or performance-indicator fields.
- Burying the answer: an intro that warms up for three sentences before saying what the page is about.
