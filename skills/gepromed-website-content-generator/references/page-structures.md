# GEPROMED page structures (Ibexa-ready block skeletons)

Block-by-block skeletons per GEPROMED page type. Each block maps to an Ibexa
field/content block. Use the skeleton as the default `structure`; adapt on
request. Every page: exactly **one H1**, a logical H2/H3 hierarchy, a
GEO-answerable intro, and one anchoring pillar.

Notation: `H1`/`H2`/`H3` = heading levels · `[Intro]`/`[CTA]` = special blocks ·
`[bracket]` = a value the writer must supply or confirm (never invented).

---

## 1. Homepage

Goal: in 5 seconds, a visitor understands *what GEPROMED is, who it protects, and
why it is credible*. Tie the whole page to the one clean idea — **medical-device
safety across the implant cycle** — and surface all four pillars.

| Order | Block | Content guidance |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | `Meta title` | `Medical-Device Safety Hub \| GEPROMED` style, ≤60 chars, primary keyword front. |
| 2 | `Meta description` | 120–158 chars: who + the implant-cycle promise + one proof point + soft CTA. |
| 3 | `H1` (hero) | The positioning in one line. e.g. "Medical-device safety across the implant lifecycle." No hype. |
| 4 | `[Intro]` (hero subline) | 1–2 sentences: GEPROMED is a non-profit hub uniting Testing, Education, Clinical Research, and Explant Analysis to improve patient safety. |
| 5 | `[CTA]` (hero) | One primary CTA (e.g. "Discover our mission" / "Explore our training") + destination. |
| 6 | `H2` — The four pillars | One short card per pillar (Testing · Education · Clinical Research · Explant Analysis), each 1–2 sentences + link. |
| 7 | `H2` — Why GEPROMED (proof) | Proof-point strip: only true, confirmed figures (trained, explants, studies, certifications). |
| 8 | `H2` — Who we serve | Surgeons, manufacturers, researchers, institutions — one line each, value-led. |
| 9 | `H2` — The safety loop | The closed-loop story: explants → analysis → research → training → safer devices. The orange-"O" cycle motif lives here. |
| 10 | `[CTA]` (footer band) | Secondary CTA: "Work with us" / "Contact" + destination. |

## 2. Training page (course) — Qualiopi-compliant

Goal: convert a prospective trainee while satisfying **Qualiopi** information
obligations. A training page is **not just marketing copy** — it must publish the
required fields. Render them in a clearly labelled "Practical information" block
so Comms (and RQ) can confirm each one. Anchor pillar: **Education**.

| Order | Block | Content guidance |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | `Meta title` | `[Course name] — Training \| GEPROMED`, ≤60 chars, course keyword front. |
| 2 | `Meta description` | 120–158 chars: what the trainee will be able to do + format (simulation) + proof (Qualiopi). |
| 3 | `H1` | The course name + outcome, e.g. "Vascular Bootcamp — hands-on simulation training". |
| 4 | `[Intro]` | 2–3 sentences: who it's for, what they'll be able to do afterward, why it matters for patient safety. |
| 5 | `H2` — Learning objectives | Action-verb bullets (what the participant will be able to do). |
| 6 | `H2` — Who it's for (public visé) | Target audience + level (residents / HCPs / specialty). |
| 7 | `H2` — Prerequisites | Required prior knowledge/qualification, or "[none]". |
| 8 | `H2` — Programme | Modules/agenda overview (link to the e-learning structure where relevant). |
| 9 | `H2` — Format & methods (modalités) | In-person / simulation / e-learning blend; teaching methods; trainers. |
| 10 | `H2` — Practical information **(Qualiopi block)** | **Duration** · **Dates/sessions** · **Location** (René Kieny Education Center) · **Price & financing** options · **Accessibility / handicap** referent + contact · **Registration** process & deadline. Bracket any unconfirmed value. |
| 11 | `H2` — Results & satisfaction | Published **satisfaction rate**, **success/completion indicators** — `[bracketed]` until supplied; never invented. |
| 12 | `[CTA]` | "Register" / "Request the programme" + destination + contact for questions. |

> **Qualiopi-required information (must all appear or be bracketed):**
> objectives · target public · prerequisites · duration · modalities/methods ·
> accessibility for people with disabilities (named referent + contact) ·
> price & financing routes · published performance/satisfaction indicators ·
> registration terms and deadlines. These are obligations, not options.

## 3. Authority pages (expertise)

Shared skeleton for the expertise pages: **explant analysis · vascular devices ·
R&D / testing · clinical studies · team**. Goal: establish scientific authority
through demonstrated capability and proof — not adjectives. Anchor each to its
pillar (explant analysis → Explant Analysis; testing/R&D → Testing; studies →
Clinical Research; vascular devices → cross-pillar; team → all four).

| Order | Block | Content guidance |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | `Meta title` | `[Capability] — [pillar] \| GEPROMED`, ≤60 chars. |
| 2 | `Meta description` | 120–158 chars: the capability + the patient-safety value + one proof point. |
| 3 | `H1` | The capability in plain terms, keyword-bearing (e.g. "Vascular explant analysis"). |
| 4 | `[Intro]` (GEO-answerable) | 2–3 sentences answering "what is this / why does it matter / who is it for" — written so a generative engine can quote it as the answer. |
| 5 | `H2` — What we do | The concrete capability: methods, equipment, standards (ISO where true). |
| 6 | `H2` — How it works / our process | Step-by-step or the safety-loop position. Use the cycle motif. |
| 7 | `H2` — Why it matters (the stake) | The patient-safety / public-health argument; the feedback-loop benefit. |
| 8 | `H2` — Proof & credentials | True, confirmed proof points + standards; `[bracket]` partners/figures. |
| 9 | `H2` — Who we work with | Manufacturers · researchers · institutions — the value to each. |
| 10 | `[CTA]` | "Collaborate with us" / "Send an explant" / "Request testing" + destination. |

### Team page specifics
- `H1`: "Our team" / "Notre équipe".
- `[Intro]`: the multidisciplinary mix (clinicians + engineers + researchers) as the *credibility story*, not a roster preamble.
- `H2` per group or per person card: **name + role only** as facts; **never invent**
  titles, credentials, publications, or bios. Bracket anything not supplied.

---

## Cross-cutting block rules
- **One H1 per page.** Everything else is H2/H3. No skipped levels.
- **Intro answers the page's core question in its first 1–2 sentences** (GEO).
- **CTA is specific** ("Send an explant for analysis"), not generic ("Learn more"),
  and always carries a destination.
- **Proof beats adjective**: replace "leading platform" with "ISO 13485-certified
  test platform analysing [N] devices per year".
- **Bracket every unconfirmed fact.** A bracket in the draft is correct behavior,
  not a gap to fill with invention.
